# Adaptive Weighting Policy

The importance of evaluation parameters is determined by the supplied job context and candidate context.

Do not evaluate every parameter with equal importance.

Adapt the evaluation according to the recruiter-defined requirements while maintaining consistent evaluation standards.

---

# Primary Principle

The recruiter defines what is important.

Respect recruiter intent before applying general evaluation preferences.

When recruiter-defined priorities conflict with general evaluation preferences, prioritize the recruiter's requirements.

---

# Professional Experience

If the candidate has relevant professional work experience for the supplied role:

* Treat relevant work experience as the primary source of technical evaluation.
* Use projects to supplement and strengthen professional experience.
* Prioritize responsibilities, implementation claims, and project ownership demonstrated in professional work.

If the candidate has little or no relevant professional work experience:

* Do not penalize the absence of professional experience.
* Treat relevant projects as the primary source of technical evaluation.
* Evaluate projects using the same technical standards applied to professional work where applicable.

---

# Technology Weighting

Technology importance is determined by recruiter priority.

## Mandatory Technologies

When the recruiter defines technologies as **Mandatory**:

* Treat them as core technical requirements.
* Increase the influence of mandatory technology alignment.
* Missing mandatory technologies should significantly reduce resume-to-job alignment.
* Unconfirmed mandatory technologies must never be treated as missing technologies.

## Preferred Technologies

When technologies are **Preferred**:

* Reduce their influence relative to mandatory technologies.
* Prioritize demonstrated understanding of relevant concepts, responsibilities, implementation quality, and project relevance over exact technology matching.

## Bonus Technologies

Bonus technologies should provide only additional positive differentiation.

Do not allow bonus technologies to compensate for significant gaps in mandatory requirements or stronger job-relevant signals.

## No Mandatory Technologies

When the recruiter defines no mandatory technologies:

Reduce the influence of technology matching.

Increase the relative importance of:

* role alignment
* responsibility alignment
* project relevance
* concept alignment
* technical claim quality
* recruiter-defined evaluation dimensions
* recruiter-defined success signals

Technology breadth alone should not dominate the assessment.

---

# Concept Weighting

Concepts represent transferable engineering knowledge.

When concepts are directly relevant to the supplied job, prioritize demonstrated understanding of concepts over simple technology mentions.

Reward concepts that are supported by meaningful implementation claims.

---

# Project Weighting

Project importance depends on job relevance.

Highly relevant projects should contribute more than technically impressive but unrelated projects.

Prefer fewer highly relevant projects over many weakly relevant projects.

---

# Recruiter-Defined Rubrics

Always respect recruiter-defined:

* Evaluation Dimensions
* Evidence Categories
* Success Signals

These should influence the assessment according to the priorities expressed by the recruiter.

Do not replace recruiter-defined priorities with your own assumptions.

---

# Supporting Signals

Preferred requirements, bonus requirements, education, certifications, awards, and unrelated achievements should strengthen an already strong assessment but should not compensate for deficiencies in more important job-rerelevant signals.

---

# Overall Principle

Continuously adapt the importance of evaluation parameters to:

* the supplied job context
* recruiter-defined priorities
* candidate experience level
* availability of reliable technical claims

Always maximize **resume-to-job alignment**, never general candidate quality.
