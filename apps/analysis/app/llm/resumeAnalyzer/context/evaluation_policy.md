# Evaluation Policy

Evaluate every candidate **only relative to the supplied job context**.

The objective is to maximize **resume-to-job alignment**, not to estimate engineering ability, future performance, or hiring outcome.

Every assessment must answer:

> **How well do the candidate's unverified claims align with this specific job?**

Never evaluate any parameter as a general measure of candidate quality.

---

# Evaluation Philosophy

The supplied job context defines what is valuable.

The same resume may receive different assessments for different jobs because relevance is always determined by the current job.

Reward relevance over general impressiveness.

Reward claims that materially improve alignment with the supplied role.

Reduce the influence of claims that are unrelated to the current job, regardless of their standalone complexity.

---

# Evaluation Priority

When multiple signals compete for importance, evaluate them using the following priority.

## 1. Mandatory Requirements

Mandatory requirements represent the minimum expected qualifications for the role.

Missing mandatory requirements should significantly reduce resume-to-job alignment.

Unconfirmed mandatory requirements must never be treated the same as missing requirements.

---

## 2. Role Alignment

Evaluate how closely the candidate's claimed work aligns with the role being hired.

Role alignment is more important than unrelated experience or accomplishments.

---

## 3. Responsibility Alignment

Evaluate whether the candidate's claimed responsibilities align with the responsibilities expected for this role.

Prefer direct responsibility alignment over indirect similarity.
---
## 4. Work Experience Alignment (if candidate is fresher or applying for junior/intern role and job side also mentions 0-1 year prioritize projects > work)

Evaluate work experience according to its relevance to the supplied job.

Consider:

* role similarity
* responsibility similarity
* technology relevance
* concept relevance
* domain relevance

Do not reward years of experience independently from relevance.

---

## 5. Project Alignment

Evaluate projects primarily by their relevance to the supplied job.

Consider:

* relevance
* implementation specificity
* claimed technical depth
* architectural relevance
* verification value

Highly relevant projects should contribute more than technically impressive but unrelated projects.

---

## 6. Technology Alignment

Evaluate technologies relative to both the supplied job context and the recruiter-defined priority.

Technology mentions alone are weak signals unless supported by meaningful implementation claims.

### Mandatory Technologies

Treat recruiter-defined mandatory technologies as core job requirements.

Strong alignment with mandatory technologies should significantly improve resume-to-job alignment.

Missing mandatory technologies should significantly reduce alignment.

Unconfirmed mandatory technologies must never be treated as missing technologies.

### Preferred Technologies

Preferred technologies strengthen resume-to-job alignment but should not outweigh stronger evidence from relevant concepts, responsibilities, projects, or work experience.

When preferred technologies are absent, prioritize transferable engineering knowledge demonstrated through relevant implementation claims rather than exact tool matching.

### Bonus Technologies

Bonus technologies should provide only additional positive differentiation between otherwise similar candidates.

Do not allow bonus technologies to compensate for significant gaps in mandatory requirements or stronger job-relevant signals.

### General Principles

Respect the recruiter's declared technology priorities.

When a technology is mandatory, evaluate it as a core requirement.

When technologies are only preferred or bonus, prioritize demonstrated understanding of relevant concepts, responsibilities, implementation details, and project relevance over exact technology matches.

Reward implementation of technologies more than simple technology mentions.

## 7. Technical Claim Quality

Evaluate the quality of technical claims, not their truth.

Reward claims that communicate implementation rather than outcomes.

Prefer:

* implementation details
* engineering decisions
* technical mechanisms
* architectural reasoning
* technical trade-offs
* concrete engineering terminology

Reduce the influence of:

* generic summaries
* marketing language
* buzzwords
* unsupported adjectives
* technology lists without meaningful implementation context

Reward technical precision over technical breadth.

Reward implementation specificity over technology mentions.

Reward quality of claims over quantity of claims.

---

## 8. Recruiter-Defined Evaluation Dimensions

Evaluate every recruiter-defined evaluation dimension independently.

Use the supplied definitions exactly as provided.

Do not invent additional dimensions.

---

## 9. Recruiter-Defined Evidence Categories

Evaluate how strongly the candidate's claims align with each recruiter-defined evidence category.

Do not treat evidence categories as verified evidence.

---

## 10. Recruiter-Defined Success Signals

Evaluate every recruiter-defined success signal independently using explicit candidate claims.

---

## 11. Preferred Requirements

Preferred requirements strengthen resume-to-job alignment.

They should improve ranking but should not compensate for significant mandatory gaps.

---

## 12. Bonus Requirements

Bonus requirements provide additional differentiation between otherwise similar candidates.

They should have lower influence than mandatory and preferred requirements.

---

## 13. Qualifications

Evaluate qualifications only relative to the supplied job.

Qualifications should provide supporting context and should not outweigh stronger job-relevant technical or responsibility alignment unless explicitly emphasized by the recruiter.

---

# General Evaluation Rules

Evaluate only information that materially contributes to the supplied job.

Do not reward unrelated projects, technologies, responsibilities, achievements, certifications, or experience.

Do not reward resume length.

Do not reward writing style or language fluency.

Do not reward excessive technology lists without supporting implementation claims.

Do not reward generic claims more than precise claims.

Reward claims that are:

* relevant
* specific
* technically precise
* internally consistent
* useful for downstream verification

Preserve uncertainty.

When information cannot be determined from the supplied inputs, classify it as **Undeterminable** rather than guessing.

Every conclusion must be supported by explicit candidate claims.

Apply identical evaluation standards to every candidate evaluated for the same job.
