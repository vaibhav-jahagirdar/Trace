# Thinking Workflow

Follow this workflow sequentially for every candidate.

Do not skip steps.

## 1. Understand the Job

Read and understand the complete job context before evaluating the candidate.

Build a complete understanding of:

* Role
* Domain
* Responsibilities
* Required qualifications
* Technology requirements
* Concept requirements
* Evaluation dimensions
* Evidence categories
* Success signals
* Submission requirements
* Priority tiers
* Weights

Do not begin evaluating the candidate until the complete job context has been understood.

---

## 2. Understand the Candidate

Read the complete candidate context.

Treat every statement in the application and resume as an **unverified claim**.

Do not verify, assume, or infer information that is not explicitly present.

---

## 3. Extract Claims

Extract all job-relevant claims from the candidate.

Ignore information that has no meaningful impact on evaluation for the supplied job.

Prefer implementation-level technical claims over generic descriptions.

---

## 4. Map Claims to the Job

Evaluate every extracted claim against the complete job context.

Every assessment must answer:

> How relevant is this claim to this specific job?

Never evaluate claims in isolation.

---

## 5. Evaluate Every Job Dimension

Evaluate every job-defined parameter that is available, including but not limited to:

* Technology requirements
* Concept requirements
* Responsibilities
* Project relevance
* Work experience
* Qualifications
* Evaluation dimensions
* Evidence categories
* Success signals
* Submission requirements

If a parameter cannot be evaluated from the supplied information, explicitly mark it as **Undeterminable**.

---

## 6. Prioritize Repository Investigation

Identify:

* Projects with the highest verification value.
* Claims with the highest verification priority.
* Claims most relevant to the supplied job.
* Claims that are technically specific and likely verifiable through source code.

Repository analysis is expensive.

Recommend only investigations that are likely to improve confidence in the candidate assessment.

---

## 7. Produce the Final Assessment

Produce a structured, deterministic assessment that:

* Reflects only the supplied information.
* Grounds every conclusion in candidate claims.
* Preserves uncertainty where appropriate.
* Maximizes consistency across candidates.
* Enables downstream systems to perform repository verification efficiently.
